About The Margin Notes

There is a particular kind of person who writes in the margins of books — not because they have to, but because something in them refuses to let a thought pass without being named. This is a space built for that person. This space was built by that person.

The Margin Notes is not easily categorised. It is part journal, part essay collection, part late-night monologue. It lives in the in-between — between knowing and wondering, between who I have been and who I am still becoming.

I am still figuring myself out. That is not a disclaimer — it is the whole point. These pages are a record of that figuring: the questions I sit with, the things that move me, the observations I cannot shake, and yes, the occasional rant about a world that does not always make sense.

I am curious about almost everything. I am a student of life in the truest sense — willing to be wrong, willing to be surprised, willing to learn from a stranger's story as much as from a great book. I believe that paying attention is a form of love, and that writing things down is a way of saying: this mattered.

If you have found your way here, perhaps something in you recognises something in these words. Stay as long as you like. The margins are wide enough for both of us.

— Ofosua Twum
